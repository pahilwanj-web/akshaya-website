The Evolution of EFT

From ancient energy meridians to modern clinical applications.

Ancient Origins

EFT's foundation lies in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), specifically the concept of energy meridians flowing through the body, established over 5,000 years ago.

1980s: Thought Field Therapy

Dr. Roger Callahan discovered that tapping on acupressure points while a patient focused on a phobia yielded rapid relief. He developed TFT, which required complex algorithms.

1995: The Birth of EFT

Gary Craig, an engineer, simplified TFT. He realized that tapping all major meridian points in a single sequence worked for almost every issue, democratizing the technique.

Present Day: Validation

Today, Clinical EFT is an evidence-based practice supported by over 100 peer-reviewed medical and psychology journals. It is utilized by therapists worldwide.

The Tapping Process

The standard EFT Basic Recipe involves 5 clear steps.

1

Identify the Issue

Choose a specific problem to focus on. Be as specific as possible (e.g., 'My fear of the upcoming presentation').

2

Test Initial Intensity

Rate the intensity of your problem on a scale from 0 to 10. This serves as a benchmark.

3

The Setup

While continuously tapping the Karate Chop point, repeat: 'Even though I have this [issue], I deeply and completely accept myself.' (3 times)

4

The Sequence

Tap on each of the 8 meridian points about 5-7 times each, while repeating a brief 'reminder phrase' (e.g., 'This presentation anxiety').

5

Test Intensity Again

Rate your problem again from 0 to 10. Repeat the process until the intensity drops to 0 or a manageable level.
